Assam Macaque

The Assam macaque is a medium-sized monkey found in the forests and hills of South and Southeast Asia, especially in countries like India, Bhutan, and Nepal. It has thick brown fur, a short tail, and a calm, social nature, often living in groups. These monkeys are adaptable, feeding on fruits, leaves, and insects, and are important for maintaining forest health by spreading seeds.

Binturong

The binturong, also known as the bearcat, is a unique, tree-dwelling mammal found in the rainforests of South and Southeast Asia. It has a thick black coat, a long prehensile tail, and a face that looks like a mix between a bear and a cat. Binturongs are mostly active at night and love to eat fruits, especially figs, and they smell like buttered popcorn!

Clouded Leopard

The clouded leopard is a medium-sized wild cat native to the forests of Southeast Asia. It has beautiful, cloud-like patterned fur that helps it blend into the trees. Known for its incredible climbing skills, this elusive predator hunts small mammals and birds, and is considered vulnerable due to habitat loss.

Dhole

The dhole, also called the Asian wild dog, is a highly social and intelligent carnivore found across forests and grasslands in Asia. It has reddish fur, sharp teeth, and hunts in packs to catch prey like deer. Dholes play an important role in balancing ecosystems but are endangered due to habitat loss and human conflict.

Elephant (Asian Elephant)

The Asian elephant is a large, gentle mammal native to the forests of South and Southeast Asia. Smaller than its African cousin, it has smaller ears and a rounded back. Known for its intelligence and strong social bonds, the Asian elephant plays a crucial role in its ecosystem but faces threats from habitat loss and poaching.

Fennec Fox

The fennec fox is a small desert fox with huge ears that help it stay cool, found mainly in the deserts of North Africa and parts of the Middle East in Asia. It’s nocturnal, uses its keen hearing to hunt insects and small animals, and is known for its adorable appearance and incredible adaptability to harsh environments.

Gaur

The gaur, also called the Indian bison, is a massive wild cattle species native to South and Southeast Asia. It has a muscular build, dark brown coat, and impressive curved horns. Gaurs are herbivores that graze in forests and grasslands and are considered vulnerable due to habitat loss and hunting.

Hog Deer

The hog deer is a small deer species found in the grasslands and forests of South and Southeast Asia. Named for its hog-like running style, it has a stocky body and short legs. Hog deer are shy herbivores that feed on grasses and leaves and are important for maintaining healthy ecosystems.

Indian Leopard

The Indian leopard is a big cat native to Nepal’s forests and hills. It has a golden-yellow coat with black rosettes, which helps it blend into the wild. Leopards are excellent climbers and hunters, feeding on deer and smaller animals. They are elusive but important predators that help keep the ecosystem balanced.

Jungle Cat

The jungle cat is a medium-sized wild cat commonly found in the forests and grasslands of Nepal. It has sandy or grayish fur, long legs, and a short tail. Jungle cats are skilled hunters, feeding on rodents, birds, and insects, and are mostly active during dawn and dusk.

Kashmir Markhor

The Kashmir markhor is a wild goat species native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and nearby areas. It has impressive twisted horns and a thick coat that helps it survive cold climates. Markhors are agile climbers and graze on grasses and shrubs, playing an important role in their alpine ecosystem.

Leopard Cat

The leopard cat is a small wild cat native to the forests and hills of Nepal. It has a spotted coat that resembles a miniature leopard, helping it camouflage in dense vegetation. Leopard cats are nocturnal hunters, feeding on rodents, birds, and insects, and they play a key role in controlling pest populations.

Musk Deer

The musk deer is a small, shy deer found in the mountainous forests of Nepal. Known for its unique tusk-like teeth that protrude from the males’ mouths, it produces musk used in perfumes. Musk deer are herbivores that feed on leaves, grasses, and flowers, and they prefer dense forest habitats.

Nagal Deer (Barasingha)

The nagal deer, also called the barasingha, is a large swamp deer native to the grasslands and wetlands of Nepal. It has a beautiful coat and distinctive antlers with multiple tines. These deer graze on grasses and aquatic plants and are important for maintaining wetland ecosystems.

One-horned Rhinoceros

The one-horned rhinoceros, also known as the Indian rhinoceros, is a large, powerful herbivore native to the grasslands and forests of Nepal, especially Chitwan National Park. It has thick, armor-like skin and a single horn on its snout. This rhino plays a crucial role in its ecosystem by shaping vegetation and creating habitats for other animals.

Pangolin

The pangolin is a unique mammal covered with hard, overlapping scales found in Nepal’s forests. It is nocturnal and feeds mainly on ants and termites using its long, sticky tongue. Pangolins are shy and solitary creatures, but unfortunately, they are threatened due to illegal poaching and habitat loss.

Quail

The quail is a small ground-dwelling bird found in various parts of Nepal, especially in grasslands and scrub forests. Quails have plump bodies, short tails, and are known for their distinctive calls and fast, low flight. They feed on seeds, insects, and small plants and are important for maintaining ecological balance.

Red Panda

The red panda is a small, tree-dwelling mammal native to the temperate forests of Nepal and the Himalayas. It has reddish-brown fur, a long bushy tail, and a cute face with white markings. Red pandas mainly eat bamboo and are shy, solitary creatures. They are considered endangered due to habitat loss.

Sand Boa

The sand boa is a non-venomous snake found in the lowland and dry regions of Nepal. It has a thick body, blunt tail, and is known for burrowing into sandy soil. Sand boas are slow-moving and harmless to humans, feeding mostly on rodents, which makes them helpful in controlling pests.

Tricarinate Hill Turtle

The tricarinate hill turtle is a small freshwater turtle found in the forest streams and rivers of Nepal. It has a distinct three-ridged (tricarinate) shell and feeds on plants, insects, and small aquatic animals. This shy reptile is threatened by habitat destruction and illegal collection.

Ussuri Catfish

The Ussuri catfish is a large freshwater fish found in rivers and lakes of northern Asia, including some river systems that extend into Nepal. It has a long body, whisker-like barbels, and a broad head. As a bottom-dweller, it feeds on smaller fish and aquatic creatures, helping to maintain balance in freshwater ecosystems.

Vampire Bat

The vampire bat, though rare in Nepal, represents a group of bats known for feeding on blood—mostly of birds or livestock. These bats are small, nocturnal, and use heat sensors and sharp teeth to make tiny bites. While the species is more common in the Americas, Nepal hosts other bat species that share similar traits and ecological roles as insect controllers.

Wolf (Indian Wolf)

The Indian wolf is a subspecies of the gray wolf found in the grasslands and open forests of southern Nepal. It has a lean body, short fur, and is highly adaptable. These wolves live and hunt in small packs, preying on deer, rodents, and livestock, and are important for maintaining predator-prey balance in the wild.

Xenophrys (Nepalese Horned Frog)

Xenophrys, now often called Megophrys, is a genus of horned frogs found in Nepal’s forested hills and mountainous regions. These frogs have camouflaged, leaf-like appearances with horn-like projections above their eyes. They blend perfectly with the forest floor and play an important role in controlling insect populations.

Yak

The yak is a long-haired, sturdy bovine native to the high-altitude Himalayan regions of Nepal. It has a thick coat, large horns, and is well adapted to cold, mountainous environments. Yaks are vital to local communities for transportation, milk, meat, and wool, and they play an important role in highland livelihoods.

Zebra Dove

The zebra dove is a small, graceful bird occasionally found in the southern lowlands of Nepal. It gets its name from the black-and-white zebra-like stripes on its neck and chest. Known for its soft, soothing cooing sound, the zebra dove feeds on seeds and small insects and is admired for its calm and gentle nature.
